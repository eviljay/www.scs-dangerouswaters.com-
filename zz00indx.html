Dangerous Waters Website Launched!
(Friday, December 19th)

After some degree of speculation, we've finally "surfaced" long enough to reveal the latest on Sonalysts Combat Simluations - "Dangerous Waters" (SCS-DW).

Below are the major sections of the new site:

NEWS - Will contain the latest information regarding the game and links to the various sections as they are updated. Also, an archive of past news headlines will be made available.

GAME FEATURES - This area will be updated as the game's major features are modified... Check it regularly.

PLATFORMS - Descriptions of each of the game's controllable platforms and the stations that will be modelled.

SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS - A baseline for a satisfactory game play experience on various system configurations.

SCREENSHOTS - Some snapshots of the new game in action. We'll try our best to update these regularly as the game evolves (hopefully, improves) and new functionality is added.

MOVIES - If the demand is there, we will update the site with some in-game footage of the game and the controllable platforms.

FORUM - Thank you to Neal Stevens and Subsim.com for hosting the "Dangerous Waters" forum. Any game related questions and commentary will be found here.

 

Official Game Press Release
(Friday, December 19th)

Below is the official press release that was submitted to industry publications and news wires, today.

Sonalysts' Combat Simulations - "Dangerous Waters": Web Site Revealed.

WATERFORD, CT - December 17th, 2003 - Sonalysts Inc., an established developer of modern naval simulation games, disclosed today the initial details of the inaugural title to their Sonalysts' Combat Simulations brand, dubbed "Dangerous Waters" (S.C.S - "Dangerous Waters"). S.C.S. - "Dangerous Waters" will be the first title of its kind, allowing the player total control over multiple air, surface and subsurface platforms in a modern-day naval environment. S.C.S. - "Dangerous Waters" allows the player control over 7 of the world's most potent naval platforms:

• Oliver Hazard Perry   [Guided Missile Frigate]
• MH-60R Seahawk     [Multi-Mission Helicopter]
• P-3C Orion              [Maritime Patrol Aircraft]
• Kilo class 636/877    [Diesel attack submarines]

• Seawolf class SSN      [Nuclear attack submarine]
• Akula I/II class SSN   [Nuclear attack submarine]
• 688(I) class SSN       [Nuclear attack submarine]

Above, below, and on the ocean's surface, S.C.S. - "Dangerous Waters" will thrust the player into battle and finally allow them to experience all facets of naval combat in one immersive gaming experience.

For more information on S.C.S. - "Dangerous Waters":
http://www.scs-dangerouswaters.com

For more information on all Sonalysts Combat Simulations:

About Sonalysts Inc.
Sonalysts began developing computer simulation games for the commercial market in the mid-nineties as part of its diversification plan following the end of the cold war. Sonalysts has developed three PC naval simulation games to date - Jane's 688(I) Hunter/Killer™, Jane's Fleet Command™, and Sub Command™. Each of these games has won a variety of awards from "Best Combat Sim of the Year" to "Best Maritime Sim" of 2001. Sonalysts' games provide the user with a scalable experience so that the most dedicated simulation and casual players can both equally enjoy them.

Sonalysts, based in Waterford, CT, is an employee-owned business with approximately 500 employees and annual sales in excess of $55M that has been serving Government and commercial clients since 1973. For more information about Sonalysts Inc, please visit http://www.sonalysts.com.
